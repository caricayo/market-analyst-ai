# 03 Character Dossiers

## Main Character
### Serin Vale
- Age: 29
- Role: Oath-cartographer and route auditor
- Public identity: Synod courier-scholar
- Private wound: Her younger brother died after she signed the permit denial that stranded his district's relief barges.

### Arc
- Start belief: "If I follow lawful process perfectly, no one can accuse me of harm."
- End belief: "A lawful system can still be violent; justice requires disobedience."

### Strengths
- Precision under pressure
- Pattern recognition in legal/magical systems
- Emotional restraint in negotiation

### Flaws
- Moral deferral to institutions
- Avoidant guilt
- Treats intimacy as liability

### Symbol
- Cracked map case inherited from her father (a flood surveyor executed for fraud he did not commit).

## Side Character
### Dame Maela Thorn
- Age: 34
- Role: Former floodgate captain, stripped of rank
- Public identity: Smuggler escort and "problem solver"
- Private wound: She obeyed an order that drowned a workers' quarter to save a Synod granary.

### Arc
- Start belief: "Mercy gets people killed; only force creates outcomes."
- End belief: "Force without accountability repeats the crime it claims to prevent."

### Strengths
- Tactical brilliance in tight spaces
- Protective loyalty
- Reads danger and motive rapidly

### Flaws
- Contempt for institutions becomes contempt for process
- Punishes herself by refusing tenderness
- Chooses speed over trust

### Symbol
- Oath-ring with the captain's crest filed off but not removed.

## Antagonist
### Provost Cael Ardin
- Age: 51
- Role: Senior legal engineer of the Lantern Synod
- Surface goal: Preserve basin stability at any cost
- True goal: Keep the Charter Engine active because collapse will expose every actor who benefited from engineered scarcity, including himself

### Antagonist Design
- Not chaotic evil; coherent utilitarian authoritarian
- Mirrors Serin's starting worldview at larger scale

## Relationship and Romance Track (Serin x Maela)
1. Stage 1: Tactical distrust
2. Stage 2: Forced interdependence
3. Stage 3: Emotional fracture after apparent betrayal
4. Stage 4: Earned trust repair through confession + shared risk
5. End state: Bond is real but unresolved by design; commitment implied, not over-written
