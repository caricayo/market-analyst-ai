# 02 World Bible

## World Name
The Basin of Asterfall

## High Concept
A mountain-ringed basin survived a centuries-old cataclysm by binding law, weather, and grain distribution to ritual oaths etched into living glass. The system ended war, then quietly became a machine that manufactures famine to preserve itself.

## Geography
1. Emberdelta
- Ash-rich farmland downstream of volcanic shelves.
- Main bread source for the basin.
- Periodic black rains sterilize fields unless warded.

2. Gloam Cliffs
- Vertical city-state of hanging bridges and oath towers.
- Seat of the Lantern Synod.

3. Mirror March
- Floodplain where glass reeds grow naturally.
- Smugglers, ferrymen, and oath-breaker enclaves.

4. The Hollow Weirs
- Giant floodgates and pressure locks under the basin.
- Hidden machinery tied to the Charter Engine.

## Factions
1. Lantern Synod
- Public ideology: "Order preserves life."
- Real leverage: controls grain authorizations via Charter seals.
- Moral compromise: engineers scarcity to justify emergency rule.

2. River Commons
- Public ideology: "Water belongs to all."
- Real leverage: canal routes and labor guilds.
- Moral compromise: weaponizes blockade and debt retaliation.

3. The Mirror Court
- Public ideology: "Truth is negotiation."
- Real leverage: intelligence, memory brokers, and transport contracts.
- Moral compromise: profits from both famine and relief.

## Oathcraft Magic System
### Rule Set (Hard Constraints)
1. Every binding requires three anchors: true name, witness, and medium.
2. Medium is almost always living glass (reedglass, towerglass, or ringglass).
3. Power returns as heat, memory-loss, or blood-debt.
4. Breaking a sworn binding rebounds proportionally to public harm prevented.

### Costs
1. Heat cost: physical fever, tremor, reduced stamina.
2. Memory cost: names, routes, or personal moments degrade.
3. Debt cost: bloodline obligations can transfer to kin.

### Taboos
1. Binding minors to civic oaths.
2. Binding without named witness.
3. Altering true names in recorded charters.

### Irreversible Consequence Class
Hollowing: permanent emotional flattening and sensory bleaching after repeated high-tier bindings.

## Economy and Pressure Model
- Grain permits are linked to oath compliance scores.
- River access gates open by charter sequence, not weather need.
- Relief convoys can be denied if local districts "fail civic tone metrics".
- This creates systemic, legal famine while preserving the appearance of lawful order.

## Social Norms
- Public speech begins with a "small oath" phrase in formal spaces.
- Marriage contracts and trade contracts use identical oath forms.
- Breaking a private promise can trigger civic penalties if witnessed and notarized.

## Technology Level
- Late-medieval civic infrastructure with ritual-engineered hydraulics.
- No firearms; blade, bow, alchemical glass charges.
- Wide literacy among contract classes, low literacy in labor wards.

## Religion
- The Tide Canon teaches that water remembers moral balance.
- Synod-aligned priests use canon selectively to legitimize ration hierarchy.

## History (Compressed Timeline)
- Year 0: Shatterfall (original catastrophe).
- Year 48: First Charter signed, ending warlord era.
- Year 183: Lantern Synod formalized as neutral authority.
- Year 227: Quiet amendments create emergency grain doctrine.
- Year 302 (present): recurrent "natural" famines intensify despite stable weather bands.

## Sequel Hooks Embedded
1. Outer treaty cities send emissaries once the basin charter collapses.
2. A dormant oath lattice under the sea shelf reactivates.
3. Surviving Synod cells flee with partial engine schematics.
